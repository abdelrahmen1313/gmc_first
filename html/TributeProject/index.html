About Hedi Jouini

Hédi Jouini (1909–1990) was a legendary Tunisian singer, composer, and oud virtuoso whose music shaped the cultural soul of Tunisia and resonated across the Arab world.
With over 1,000 songs and 56 operettas to his name, Jouini blended Andalusian musical traditions with Tunisian heritage, creating timeless melodies that continue to inspire generations. Born in Tunis, he began his musical journey at a young age, mastering the oud and composing songs that captured the joys and sorrows of everyday life.

His first major success came in the 1930s, and by the mid-20th century, he was celebrated as the "father of Tunisian popular music."
Jouini's influence extended beyond music; he was a cultural icon who represented Tunisian identity during a time of significant social and political change. His career spanned radio, film, and live performance, earning him national honors and a lasting place in Tunisia’s artistic legacy.
